Redesign Light

  • Type: Pathway Lighting System
  • Target Audience: Any person that needs to walk through dark pathways or tunnels at night.
  • Objective: Provide a adequately lit pathways/tunnels at night to be user-friendly & eco-friendly.
  • Main Feature: Lights that follow users.
  • Prototype Components: Ultra-sound sensor, Arduino, LEDs, Wires, Resistors, Breadboard, Paper
  • Team Members: David, Kristian

Overview

This project's topic is "Redesign Light". We have made some observations around us and we have found out that the lighting for some paths are too dark or the lighting for tunnels are too bright at night. We found out that these bright tunnels are not environment-friendly nor user-friendly, hence we have came up with a design to illuminate the path according to the user's position in the tunnel.

First Iteration

The first iteration is a paper prototype with 10 red LEDs and an ultra-sound sensor connected to an arduino. The ultra-sound detects the position of the target and lights up 1 LED light above it while the rest are off.

Second and Final Iteration

After testing out our first iteration with a few UST students and collecting their response, we collated the results and made the following changes accordingly:

  • Changed red LEDs to yellow LEDs to give a warmer colour and feel. Makes the visibility better as well as give the users a sense of security.
  • Changed the number of LEDs lit up from 1 to 3. Provides more vision to the users of their surrounding and path ahead.

Final Thoughts and Review

With enough ultra-sound sensors, a better design could have been made with a sensor at every LED area. Each sensor will be responsible for one LED. This design will support multiple users and would have been a better final product.

Due to limited resources, we could not implement one of our other designs that we have thought of which is to have the tunnel lighted up all the time but only dimly. The ultra-sound sensor will still pick up the user's location and brighten up the LEDs near that position. As the initial survey shows, people are less likely to approach any dark paths if there are alternatives. Hence, a dimly lit tunnel will give the users a better sense of safety while still trying to be eco-friendly.

Admittedly, our design may not work in Hong Kong as it is a very safe city. There are many other aspects to consider if such design is to be employed elsewhere in the world, such as beggers/homeless people who stays/lurks in the tunnels.
